Hardwood

As the name suggests, hardwood means hard, heavy wood from trees that lose their leaves in winter namely, deciduous trees. The trees from which hardwood is obtained tend to be slower growing, meaning the wood is usually denser which gives them enhanced durability and strength. They're suitable for a large range of applications including; construction, joinery, high-quality furniture and flooring. Along with adding value to your home, hardwood is also low maintenance and easy to clean. Adding to the advantages that hardwood offers, it is long lasting and cost effective and the colour of the wood does not fade for a foreseeable period of time. We specialise in 5 types of hardwood namely White Ash, White Oak, Walnut, Poplar and Red Oak.

Softwood

Eponymous to its characteristics, softwood is any wood that is relatively soft and easy to cut. Softwood comes from conifer plant species, which usually remains evergreen. Softwood is easier to work with and thus, can be used across a broad range of applications. The timbers are cheaper and easier to source that's why these woods are less in cost. Offering a wide range of end uses, softwood is a versatile product. It is widely used as framing (construction) material, furniture building, and paper products. We specialise in Southern Yellow Pine, which is strong, stiff, and dense. It also has the ability to hold nails and other fasteners particularly well, which makes it a great choice for residential and commercial construction.
